Research

My research includes computer vision, computational pathology, and EHR modeling, with an emphasis on learning reliable and interpretable multimodal representations for medical decision-making. I develop temporal models for clinical decision support using longitudinal EHR trajectories, alongside generative modeling approaches for scalable and uncertainty-aware computational pathology pipelines.

Projects

EHR Time-series Modeling (Clinical Decision Support)

We develop temporal models for ICU decision support using large-scale electronic health records (eICU, MIMIC-III, and MIMIC-IV), with an emphasis on early-stage prognosis, time-aware evaluation, and policy learning under clinically grounded constraints. My work introduces deep neural networks for retrospective analysis of antibiotic prescribing decisions and integrates reinforcement learning with KL-constrained exploration and off-policy evaluation. These models enable the identification and validation of empirically grounded treatment policies using previously unseen 24‚Äì48 hour clinical trajectories, with the goal of improving patient outcomes without compromising antimicrobial stewardship principles.

Computational Staining (Generative Modeling in Pathology)

We develop image-to-image translation pipelines for computational staining and destaining of prostate core tissue images using generative adversarial networks. This work includes large-scale patch generation and reconstruction workflows for high-resolution whole-slide images, enabling non-destructive virtual H&E staining from unstained or autofluorescence inputs. The pipelines are quantitatively validated using structural similarity, correlation, and signal-to-noise metrics, and integrate uncertainty estimation and region-level interpretability to support expert review. Together, this connects generative modeling with scalable, clinically grounded digital pathology workflows.

Video Understanding (Action Recognition & Video Captioning)

I develop innovative models for video-based understanding, including action recognition, video captioning, and sign language translation, leveraging CNNs, Transformers, CLIP-based models, and LLM-driven semantic reasoning. My work focuses on image and video processing, spatiotemporal representation learning, multimodal alignment, and multimodal interaction. In video captioning, large language models are used to infer enriched semantic relations across visual, auditory, and textual modalities, improving the recognition and description of subtle and complex actions and events in videos.
